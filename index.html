About Me

I am a senior at University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) as a Computer Science major and will be graduating spring 2023. My history with Computer Science and Web Development dates back to when I was in high school where I founded the Programming club. As the founder, I presided over 20 active members, taught members how to build websites with HTML, how to build bluetooth-controlled cars with Arduino, and various applications with C++.

I am a transfer student from Mt. San Antonio College where I was a student-athlete and a tutor. As a student, I was able to take and complete classes such as Data Structures and Algorithms, Linear Algebra, Multivariable Calculus, Differential Equations, Engineering Physics Mechanics, Electricity, Special Relativity , Optics and more. As an athlete, I swam ... and was the SCCCAA Men's Swimming Champion of over 12 Colleges in Southern California. As a tutor, I am in charge of assisting and mentoring over 60,000 college students with their studies, tutoring Computer Science, Calculus, and Engineering Physics Mechanics.

Aside from swimming competitively, I was a Water Safety Instructor(swim teacher) and lifeguard at my high school during the summer. Furthermore, I was also a certified SCUBA diver and was involved in Turtle and Coral Reef Conservation in Malaysia where we SCUBA dive to plant and rebuild artificial coral reefs and collect sea turtle eggs to protect them from real life poachers, crabs, snakes, and other predators. This is because like the orangutan is to its rainforest, sea turtles are rapidly disappearing and currently, I am taking classes on Marine Biology to learn more about the coral reefs and other sea animals so that I have a better understanding about the ocean that I want to protect.

During my free time, I like to play badminton, table tennis, and other sports. I also enjoy playing guitar and video games.
